\begin{abstract}
Ecological models often return a single occupancy state, mechanism, or management prediction even when finite observations support several target-relevant explanations. We introduce a modelling workflow that separates four tasks: defining candidate ecological worlds, mapping experiments to complete observation records, determining the sharpest target report justified by each compatible class, and selecting additional experiments under explicit error and cost constraints. The framework complements rather than replaces occupancy models: imperfect-detection models quantify uncertainty about occurrence, whereas our target-safe analysis asks whether all worlds compatible with the fitted observation model imply the same management conclusion. When they do not, the exact output is set-valued. A unique coarsest target-safe quotient removes distinctions irrelevant to the declared decision while preserving distinctions needed under future actions. Failure architecture is included explicitly, so replication sharing a common weather, access, observer, camera, or laboratory failure cannot create fictitious resolution. An exact 216-row benchmark crosses three detection levels, three response-typing accuracies, three common-failure levels, two failure architectures, and four reporting strategies. Under a common-failure probability of 0.35, the best correct deterministic reporting probability of the target-safe strategy is capped at 0.354 for shared-mode replication but reaches 0.514 when failures are diversified across independent modes. Across the grid its wrong deterministic reporting probability remains below the declared 0.05 contract, with unresolved cases reported explicitly. The framework is implemented as a finite, auditable software workflow for ecological model comparison, monitoring design, and management reporting.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Ecological management rarely requires identifying every biological detail. It requires a defensible answer to a declared question: is a focal interaction present, will an intervention increase or decrease it, or is the evidence insufficient to choose? Standard modelling workflows often blur these tasks. A fitted occupancy probability may be interpreted as a management conclusion; the most probable mechanism may be reported even when alternatives imply opposite interventions; and additional sampling may be selected to maximize generic information rather than reduce uncertainty in the decision that matters.

The problem is not simply imperfect detection. Nondetection can mask presence, but even perfect detection of presence may leave intervention response unresolved. Nor is it only partial identification: an identified set describes what remains compatible, but ecological monitoring also needs a reproducible rule for which distinctions matter for the target, how future actions refine them, and when data collection should stop. Adaptive monitoring addresses sequential design, yet often optimizes information gain, parameter precision, or state estimation rather than an explicit contract on correct, wrong, and ambiguous management reports.

We therefore present a finite modelling framework organized around the target report rather than full latent-state recovery. The user declares candidate ecological worlds, experiments, observation and failure contracts, future actions, costs, and the target. Each experiment partitions worlds by complete records. Deterministic reporting is valid exactly when the target is constant within the compatible class; otherwise the exact conclusion is the corresponding set of target values. The framework then constructs the unique coarsest observation-preserving, action-stable abstraction retaining every distinction required for present and future target reports. Finally, candidate adaptive policies are compared by correct-report probability, false-resolution probability, ambiguity, and expected cost.

The contribution is a modelling workflow connecting ideas that are usually applied separately. We do not propose a replacement occupancy estimator, claim novelty for set-valued inference, or present a universal adaptive-control algorithm. Instead, we show how occupancy, structural uncertainty, partial identification, failure-aware monitoring, and adaptive design can be assembled into one auditable decision model. The distinctive empirical prediction is not merely that more uncertainty should be reported. It is that monitoring designs with equal nominal replication can have sharply different target-resolution ceilings when their failure factors differ.

\section{Relationship to existing ecological modelling approaches}
\subsection{Occupancy and imperfect detection}
Occupancy models separate ecological state from observation and estimate occurrence when detection probability is below one. Our workflow can take fitted occupancy and detection models as inputs. The distinction is in the output question: occupancy asks how probable presence is, whereas target-safe reporting asks whether all candidate worlds compatible with the observation model imply the same declared management report. Presence may be well resolved while intervention response remains unresolved. In the benchmark, the occupancy-only rule therefore returns a set of response directions after detection rather than pretending that occurrence determines intervention effect.

\subsection{Partial identification}
Partial-identification methods correctly emphasize that evidence and assumptions may support sets rather than points. We adopt that reporting discipline. Our additional object is a finite experiment- and target-relative quotient that removes observable differences irrelevant to the management target while retaining differences that alter reports under declared future actions. Set-valued reporting itself is not claimed as new.

\subsection{Adaptive monitoring and experimental design}
Adaptive monitoring selects future observations conditional on past records. Generic criteria such as entropy reduction or expected information gain reward distinctions anywhere in the latent state space. Our policies are evaluated directly on target outcomes: correct deterministic reports, wrong deterministic reports, honest ambiguity, and cost. Stopping with a set-valued report is permitted when further resolution is too costly or violates the error contract. The benchmark deliberately gives information-gain monitoring the same observation model and replication budget, but it forces a maximum-prior conclusion after the budget is exhausted; this isolates the consequence of different stopping semantics rather than differences in data quality.

\subsection{Structural uncertainty and model averaging}
Multimodel approaches rank or average candidate mechanisms. Our framework instead groups mechanisms according to whether their differences can change the declared target report. Posterior concentration is informative but does not by itself justify deterministic reporting when low-probability compatible worlds imply a different management action under the declared robustness contract.

\section{Modelling framework}
A finite ecological evidence problem contains a latent-world set $W$, experiment family $\mathcal{D}$, observation contract, failure-factor architecture, finite action grammar, costs, and target map $T:W\to Y$. For a design $D$, $\operatorname{record}_D(w)$ is the complete record generated under world $w$.

\begin{definition}[Experiment-induced quotient]
For a design $D$,
\[
w\sim_D w' \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \operatorname{record}_D(w)=\operatorname{record}_D(w').
\]
The quotient $W/{\sim_D}$ is the experiment-induced ecological quotient.
\end{definition}

Under bounded or probabilistic observation error, record $x$ induces a compatible set
\[
C_D(x)=\{w\in W:x\text{ is allowed under }w\text{ and }D\}.
\]
The exact target report is $T(C_D(x))=\{T(w):w\in C_D(x)\}$.

\begin{theorem}[Honest target reporting]
A deterministic record-based report exists if and only if $T$ is constant on every relevant compatible class. Otherwise the sharp exact report is set-valued. Refining a design can split existing classes but cannot justify distinctions absent from its complete records.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Unique coarsest target-safe quotient]
For a declared finite evidence problem, there exists a unique coarsest equivalence relation that preserves current observable macrostates, preserves target reports under every declared future action, and is stable under action successors. Every other observation-preserving, action-stable quotient supporting the same target conclusions refines it.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proposition}[Failure-controlled refinement]
The reliability of a target-relevant class split is bounded by the probability that all observation modes required for that split are operational. Replication confined to a common failure factor cannot exceed its availability ceiling. Independent or partially overlapping modes can support refinements unavailable to nominally larger but failure-coupled designs.
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite risk-limited adaptive design]
For a finite policy family and declared constraints on correct-report probability, false-resolution probability, ambiguity, and expected cost, all policies can be evaluated exactly. A least-cost feasible policy exists whenever the feasible set is nonempty. Infeasible terminal records retain an honest set-valued report rather than forcing resolution.
\end{theorem}

\section{Software implementation}
The reference implementation represents worlds and records with ordinary Python objects. A minimal analysis is:

\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python]
from ced.experiment_quotient import ExperimentInducedQuotient

worlds = ((0, 0), (0, 1), (1, 0), (1, 1))

def target(w):
    presence, response = w
    return "absent" if not presence else ("decrease" if response == 0 else "increase")

screen = ExperimentInducedQuotient.from_functions(
    worlds,
    lambda w: "detected" if w[0] else "not-detected",
    target,
)

print(screen.exact_report_for_record("detected"))
# {'decrease', 'increase'}: presence is resolved, response is not.
\end{lstlisting}

The reproducible pipeline exports quotient classes, compatible targets, refinement relations, shared-failure witnesses, and policy metrics as machine-readable JSON and CSV. The benchmark is evaluated by exact enumeration rather than Monte Carlo sampling, so every reported contrast is reproducible without simulation error.

\section{Simulation benchmark}
\subsection{Scenario and factorial design}
Each site belongs to one of four worlds combining interaction absence/presence and, when present, decrease/increase response to a floral intervention. Presence screens have imperfect sensitivity and no false positives. Response typing is informative only when the interaction is present. Three screens are available. Under the shared architecture, all screens can be disabled by one weather or access failure; under the independent architecture, each screen carries its own failure event. We cross detection sensitivity $\{0.60,0.80,0.95\}$, response-typing accuracy $\{0.85,0.95,0.99\}$, and common-failure probability $\{0,0.15,0.35\}$. Combined with two failure architectures and four strategies, the benchmark contains 216 exact strategy--scenario evaluations.

\subsection{Compared strategies}
All strategies see the same finite worlds and observation probabilities. \emph{Occupancy-only} uses one presence screen, reports absence after nondetection, and leaves response direction unresolved after detection. \emph{Full identification} uses one screen and forces a typed response following detection. \emph{Information gain} continues screening up to the common budget and then forces the maximum-prior target if no detection occurs. \emph{Target-safe risk-limited} uses the same sequential screens but reports a deterministic response only when the declared response-typing error does not exceed 0.05; otherwise it retains the exact target set.

\subsection{Evaluation metrics}
Primary metrics are wrong deterministic report probability, correct deterministic report probability, honest ambiguity probability, and expected cost. The key design contrast is the correct-report ceiling under shared versus independent failures. This contrast holds the nominal replication budget fixed and changes only the architecture linking observations to failure factors.

\subsection{Results}
Across all 54 target-safe architecture--parameter combinations, wrong deterministic reporting never exceeds 0.0055, below the declared 0.05 risk contract. When response-typing accuracy is 0.85, the target-safe strategy makes no wrong deterministic response report because it abstains between increase and decrease. In contrast, full-identification and information-gain rules convert response-typing error or exhausted nondetection budgets into point conclusions.

The most important result concerns failure architecture. At common-failure probability 0.35, the best target-safe correct deterministic reporting probability under three shared-mode replicates is 0.354. With the same nominal number of screens but independent failure events, the best value is 0.514. Thus the gain of 0.160 is produced by failure diversification rather than increased sample size. Same-mode replication cannot cross the common-cause availability ceiling; changing the observation architecture can.

These results meet the primary falsification criterion: the framework changes a monitoring recommendation. Under high shared failure, buying more copies of the same observation mode cannot deliver the target-resolution probability achieved by diversifying modes or failure factors. The remaining submission benchmark is empirical calibration: replacing the illustrative probability grid with estimates from a realistic plant--pollinator monitoring design.

\section{Integrated plant--pollinator example}
Consider a rare plant--pollinator interaction monitored across sites. The target is not simply occurrence but the response to floral enhancement, exclusion, or competitor removal. Passive monitoring leaves all worlds compatible. A presence screen separates absence from presence but leaves increase and decrease mechanisms together. A response experiment separates those mechanisms only after presence is established, because an intervention record has no mechanism-typing meaning in an absent world.

Failure architecture changes the decision. Repeating cameras through the same storm window or processing all samples in one laboratory batch may raise nominal replication while preserving a shared failure ceiling. Diversifying access windows, sensor modes, observers, or laboratory batches can create a reliable target-relevant split. The exact benchmark shows that this is not a verbal warning: at failure probability 0.35, independent modes improve the best correct deterministic reporting probability from 0.354 to 0.514 without increasing the number of screens. The recommended adaptive policy therefore screens for presence, triggers response typing only after a compatible positive record, and stops when the response is resolved within the false-resolution budget or when further sampling is not cost-effective. Terminal output is one of four scientifically distinct states: absence, increase, decrease, or unresolved increase/decrease.

\section{Discussion}
The main advance is a modelling discipline: ecological models should report only distinctions supported jointly by the experiment, observation process, failure architecture, and declared target. This discipline prevents three common substitutions: treating occupancy resolution as management-response resolution, treating posterior preference as logical reportability, and treating nominal replication as independent evidence.

The benchmark identifies a concrete management consequence. When failures are shared, increasing replication count can leave target reportability almost unchanged; when failure factors are diversified, the same nominal effort can cross the resolution threshold. Monitoring design should therefore record and model failure-factor membership---weather window, access route, observer, sensor platform, extraction batch, or laboratory pipeline---rather than treating all replicate observations as exchangeable Bernoulli trials.

The framework is deliberately modular. Occupancy, N-mixture, latent-class, mechanistic, Bayesian, or ensemble models can provide probabilities over worlds and records. The target-safe layer then determines which conclusions those models jointly support and how monitoring should proceed. This makes the approach complementary to established ecological modelling rather than competitive with any one estimator.

The finite formulation is a strength for auditability but a limitation for scale. Candidate worlds, actions, and failure factors must be declared; continuous parameter spaces require discretization or approximation; and results are conditional on model adequacy. The software should therefore expose sensitivity analyses over world sets, thresholds, and failure assumptions rather than presenting one quotient as model-free truth.

For Ecological Modelling, the decisive evidence is practical: a reproducible workflow, clear contrast with occupancy-only and generic adaptive design, and a simulation result that changes a management conclusion. The theorems guarantee internal sharpness, but the ecological contribution is the ability to diagnose when more replication is useless and which type of monitoring diversity can restore target resolution.

\section{Methods and reproducibility}
The quotient is constructed by grouping worlds with identical complete records. Target-safe minimization uses finite monotone refinement by current observations, target reports under future actions, and successor stability. Policy evaluation enumerates every latent world and observation path, weights it by the declared world and record probabilities, classifies the terminal output as correct, wrong, or ambiguous, and accumulates expected cost. The parameter grid, JSON summary, CSV rows, and manuscript-ready LaTeX table are generated by \texttt{scripts/simulate\_paper\_b\_benchmark.py}. Automated tests verify probability normalization, compliance with the false-resolution contract, and the prediction that independent observation modes break the shared-failure resolution ceiling. The supplement separates proofs and implementation details from the ecological modelling narrative.
