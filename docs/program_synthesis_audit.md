# Publication synthesis audit: CCOC/RACH, MLTR, CED, and MRM

## Decision summary

The four repositories form one coherent research program, but they should not be
collapsed into one codebase or one manuscript at the present stage.

| Repository | Central uncertainty | Strongest publication identity | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| CCOC / RACH | whether an exact closed-context compression survives a widened open-composition grammar | open-composition obstruction theorem | keep frozen and independent |
| MLTR | whether an exact source macro-law survives non-nested ecological replacement | canonical relative repair and transport defect | keep independent and finish manuscript |
| CED | what finite evidence remains defensible when closure is not finitely certifiable and detection is imperfect | risk-limited partial evidence design | compress to four main results and finish manuscript |
| MRM | what may be predicted when retained mechanisms disagree about future interventions | mechanism-robust report and minimal candidate-safe quotient | keep independent and finish manuscript |

The recommended architecture is therefore four related papers, not one omnibus
paper. A future synthesis theorem may connect MLTR and MRM, but it should not delay
completion of the current programs.

## Shared research program

All four projects study when an ecological macro-description remains valid after a
previously suppressed distinction becomes operationally relevant.

```text
CCOC / RACH: a wider future grammar exposes hidden exterior distinctions.
MLTR: replacement exposes distinctions that split an inherited target fiber.
MRM: alternative mechanisms expose response-type distinctions.
CED: finite and imperfect observation exposes limits on what distinctions can be certified.
```

The common principle is:

> A coarse ecological law is justified only relative to the distinctions that the
> declared future actions and observation process can operationally expose.

This shared principle is suitable for cross-citation and a program-level overview,
but each repository has a different mathematical object and a different headline
question.

## Why CCOC / RACH should remain independent

CCOC is a frozen publication and provenance archive. Its active claim concerns
exact interfaces for one fixed finite system under a widened composition grammar.
Its headline novelty is the failure of closed-context exact compression to imply a
small exact interface for the open union grammar.

Merging active successor mathematics back into CCOC would:

- violate the declared freeze boundary;
- blur the publication claim;
- mix fixed-system open composition with replacement, mechanism ambiguity, and
  imperfect observation; and
- weaken the stable citation target.

CCOC should instead serve as Paper 1 and the conceptual source of the shared
"operationally exposed distinctions" principle.

## MLTR assessment

### Strongest result

The strongest and most distinctive MLTR contribution is not the basic transport
criterion alone. It is the construction of the coarsest exact target refinement
that preserves every inherited source merge still possible, together with the
relative transport defect.

This turns failure of portability into a unique minimal repair problem. The
replacement relation may be non-nested, and the target state space need not contain
the source state space.

### Main-text hierarchy

1. Exact transport and derived target labels — foundation.
2. Coarsest relative exact repair and transport defect — headline theorem.
3. Accumulating defect family — sharp witness.
4. Path-label coherence — main extension.
5. Minimum history augmentation — completion proposition.

### Ecological importance

MLTR addresses a central ecological difficulty: communities change membership,
interaction structure, and intervention repertoires. A coarse law calibrated before
turnover may no longer distinguish configurations that become ecologically relevant
after colonization, extinction, habitat change, or rewiring. The transport defect
provides a principled measure of how much additional macrostate resolution is
required to retain the original ecological semantics.

### Publication decision

Keep MLTR independent. It already has a coherent transport-to-repair-to-history
story. Adding MRM or CED wholesale would replace that clear story with several
unrelated uncertainty layers.

## MRM assessment

### Strongest results

The strongest MRM sequence is:

1. report trichotomy: universal deterministic, typed deterministic, or set-valued;
2. minimal candidate-safe quotient;
3. canonical mechanism-ambiguity frontier;
4. shortest and minimum-cost active discrimination.

These results directly answer when a deterministic ecological prediction is
licensed despite unresolved mechanism uncertainty.

The bounded-error update, Bayesian update, and one-step value-of-information
modules are useful, but they should be presented as extensions or applied design
sections rather than equal headline theorems.

### Ecological importance

Ecologists routinely retain multiple plausible mechanisms that agree with current
patterns but predict different responses to intervention. MRM formalizes a reporting
standard: mechanism uncertainty may be collapsed only when all retained response
types agree on the declared future actions. Otherwise the forecast must retain the
relevant type distinction or remain set-valued or posterior-ambiguous.

The minimal quotient is especially important because it avoids the opposite error:
retaining every raw mechanism label even where it has no predictive consequence.

### Publication decision

Keep MRM independent. Its strongest story is not imperfect detection or replacement;
it is honest prediction under unresolved structural mechanism ambiguity.

## CED assessment

### Current problem

CED contains many valid theorem layers, but fifteen numbered results are too many
for one strong manuscript. Several results are refinements of a common design
principle rather than independent discoveries.

### Recommended compression

CED should be written around four main results:

1. **Finite closure non-certifiability.** No uniform passive finite horizon
   certifies closure over delayed-exposure families.
2. **Exact partial evidence quotient.** A declared intervention panel recovers
   exactly the coordinates it probes and leaves a quantified residual class.
3. **Robust detection under imperfect and shared failure.** Detection guarantees
   depend separately on repeats, independent modes, overlapping failure factors,
   and resetability.
4. **Calibrated risk-limited evidence design.** False-positive thresholds,
   calibration bounds, multiple-coordinate budgets, adaptive alpha spending, and
   independent concentration bounds form one risk-accounting framework.

### Main-text versus supplement

| Current CED result | Placement |
|---|---|
| delayed exposure / no uniform horizon | main theorem 1 |
| exact panel quotient and resource frontiers | main theorem 2 |
| one-sided imperfect detection | main theorem 3 foundation |
| independent mode diversity | main theorem 3 |
| overlapping latent failure factors | main theorem 3 extension |
| dependent / non-reset repeats | main theorem 3 boundary case |
| bounded false-positive threshold evidence | main theorem 4 foundation |
| calibration-derived error bounds | main theorem 4 |
| multiple-coordinate and heterogeneous thresholds | main theorem 4 corollaries |
| expected false-discovery budget | main theorem 4 corollary |
| adaptive alpha spending | main theorem 4 adaptive extension |
| independent Poisson-binomial / Chernoff concentration | supplement or final corollary |
| ordinary FDR | do not add unless demanded by the final manuscript |

### Ecological importance

CED's ecological contribution is a distinction between absence of evidence and a
finite evidence certificate. Passive non-detection cannot generally establish
closure, while an explicit intervention and observation design can support partial,
error-bounded conclusions. This is directly relevant to biodiversity monitoring,
pathogen surveillance, interaction detection, eDNA panels, camera networks, and
management experiments where shared weather, access, hardware, or observer failures
make nominal replication misleading.

### Publication decision

Keep CED independent and stop adding theorem families by default. The remaining
work is manuscript consolidation, formal proof writing, figures, ecological worked
examples, and literature positioning.

## Pairwise merger assessment

### CCOC + MLTR

Do not merge. They are complementary but mathematically distinct:

- CCOC widens the future grammar within one system.
- MLTR moves a law across a non-nested replacement relation.

Cross-citation is sufficient.

### CCOC + CED

Do not merge. CCOC supplies the exact-compression obstruction that motivates CED,
but CED changes the question from representation to finite evidence and monitoring
risk. A bridge paragraph or proposition is enough.

### CCOC + MRM

Do not merge. The archived joint exterior-mechanism lower bound is useful provenance,
but MRM's publication identity is mechanism-safe prediction rather than open
composition.

### CED + MRM

Do not merge the current manuscripts. Both treat uncertainty and intervention
design, but their hidden variables differ:

- CED: whether a coordinate is present and detectable under an observation design;
- MRM: which response mechanism determines future macro transitions.

Combining all current results would produce an oversized manuscript with two
openings, two state spaces, and two distinct meanings of evidence. The useful
connection is narrower: CED-style observation contracts can later be used inside
MRM discrimination experiments.

### MLTR + MRM

This is the strongest future synthesis candidate, but not an immediate merger.
Both operate on exact macro-laws and quotient/refinement constructions. A genuinely
new synthesis would require candidate uncertainty over replacement relations or
replacement mechanisms.

Potential future theorem program:

1. each candidate replacement relation induces a candidate-specific carried
   partition and minimal repair;
2. a universal transported law exists exactly when all candidate repaired
   transition tables agree;
3. otherwise the minimal candidate-safe transport quotient is the coarsest common
   observation-preserving refinement across candidate repairs; and
4. interventions may actively discriminate replacement mechanism or path class.

This would be a new paper, not a reason to merge the current MLTR and MRM manuscripts.

## Novelty positioning

The program should not claim novelty merely because it uses ecology terminology.
The defensible novelty lies in exact finite decision structures and minimality
statements:

- CCOC: exact memory obstruction under open composition;
- MLTR: source-relative coarsest exact repair after non-nested replacement;
- MRM: minimal candidate-safe deterministic quotient under mechanism disagreement;
- CED: explicit separation of non-certifiable closure from risk-limited partial
  evidence under failure and observation contracts.

Existing ecological forecasting, adaptive management, occupancy modelling,
Bayesian model averaging, and value-of-information methods already address model
uncertainty, imperfect observation, and measurement choice. The manuscripts should
therefore avoid claiming invention of uncertainty-aware ecology. Their novelty is
instead the exact structural criterion specifying when information may be safely
forgotten, when it must be retained, and what the unique minimal repair or honest
report is under a declared finite contract.

## Endgame publication plan

### Paper 1 — CCOC / RACH

Status: frozen theorem archive. Do not reopen except for correction, release, and
citation maintenance.

### Paper 2 — MLTR

Status: mathematically mature and closest to submission. Finish formal proofs,
figures, related work, release policy, and stable archival tag.

### Paper 3 — CED

Status: theorem inventory complete. Freeze theorem expansion after manuscript-core
compression. Write one integrated paper around non-certifiability, partial quotient,
robust detection, and calibrated risk.

### Paper 4 — MRM

Status: mathematically mature. Focus the paper on report trichotomy, minimal
candidate-safe quotient, ambiguity frontier, and active discrimination. Move
observation and VOI refinements behind the central story.

### Optional Paper 5 — mechanism-robust transport

Do not begin until Papers 2–4 have complete manuscripts. This is the proper place
for the MLTR × MRM synthesis theorem.

## Immediate actions

1. Stop default theorem expansion in CED and MRM.
2. Create a CED manuscript architecture based on four main results.
3. Keep MLTR's existing five-level hierarchy and complete the formal manuscript.
4. Tighten MRM's manuscript to four headline contributions and demote secondary
   statistical/design extensions.
5. Use one shared terminology table across papers: exact macro-law, declared
   grammar, operational distinction, quotient, refinement, repair, response type,
   observation contract, and non-claim.
6. Write one program overview figure for talks and introductions, but not as a
   substitute for the individual manuscript narratives.
